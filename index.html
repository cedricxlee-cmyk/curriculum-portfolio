Research Background

AI Fort Inspector

The curriculum in this portfolio grew from active research. AI Fort Inspector is a trust calibration tool built for students ages 8–14 — a Stanford Create+AI Challenge finalist — examining how learners, particularly neurodivergent ones, calibrate trust in AI outputs.

Stanford Create+AI · Finalist
AI Fort Inspector
v8 — Training Arc
Students inspect AI outputs for six documented failure patterns — Coherence Trap, Precision Illusion, Authority Override, Plausible Drift, Hedge Burial. The tool is the research prototype that directly informed the Unit 2 AI Trust Trial simulation and the ORACLE Investigation game.
Launch Inspector v8 ↗
Trust Calibration Research
Active study with Visions In Education, UC Berkeley (Bird Sellergren), and Canterbury Christ Church University (Dr. Manish Malik) on how neurodivergent learners calibrate AI trust.
Six Failure Patterns
Coherence Trap · Precision Illusion · Authority Override · Plausible Drift · Hedge Burial · Edge Case Brittleness — each mapped to curriculum interventions in Unit 2.
Direct Curriculum Connection
The AI Trust Trial simulation embedded in Unit 2 and the ORACLE Investigation game both operationalize the failure patterns identified in AI Fort research.
Neurodiversity Focus
The research specifically examines how autistic and ADHD learners interact differently with AI confidence signals — with direct implications for Unit 2 differentiation design.
Why This Portfolio

What online learning
actually requires.

Every design decision in this portfolio was made with Visions' specific context in mind: TK–12 span, nine-county reach, family learning coaches, independent study model, and a student population that is already living inside the digital environments these units examine.

What makes these online-native
  • Family coaches are designed instructional partners — not supervisors
  • Students' actual devices and feeds are the primary data sources
  • Nine-county distribution is the engine of Unit 3, not a limitation
  • Assessment is portfolio and behavior-based, not test-based
  • Games run entirely in the browser — no installation, no login
  • Cross-grade collaboration is structural, not supplementary
What the motivation design solves
  • Disengaged students cannot fake the AI Trust Trial score
  • TK-2 Passport creates social stakes through coach visibility
  • Archive selection by 9-12 editors makes younger students' work matter
  • Carmen-style games embed learning in the mechanic, not the quiz
  • Editorial accountability makes 9-12 reasoning visible to peers
  • Verification Rate Tracker catches over-acceptance behavior
Standards alignment
  • CA Teaching Credential — Social Science
  • ISTE Digital Citizenship & Empowered Learner standards
  • AI4K12 Big Ideas 4 & 5
  • California ELA standards K–12
  • CASEL Self-Awareness & Social Awareness
  • Common Sense Media K–12 framework
